Who am I?

Welcome to Logan Zen Design, I am a very focused and self-motivated individual who is very passionate about design. I achieved a GCSE & GNVQ in Art and Design. I also have a Bachelor’s Degree in creative digital media and been teaching myself Graphic Design for a couple of years now and love everything from logo design to advertising. I really enjoy looking at typography and how to tell a story with words. I always look at logos and designs in the world around me and think about how I may have solved the brief if I was given it, I am constantly thinking about design, and it’s my obsession. I work great in a team and also well on my own. I am a great communicator and have great customer service skills so could create great rapport with clients. What I lack with industry experience I make up for with my passion, desire and want to learn everything about the industry. I am like a sponge constantly absorbing new information and continuously wanting to learn.

Skills

Started using originally in 1998 when I was doing GNVQ Art and Design at College, I used it to create a digital booster pack of Magic The Gathering Cards. I left college in 2001 and after a short hiatus from education I started attending University in 2004 where I started using photoshop again, this time it was to create seamless textures for games design. I continue to learn Photoshop and improve my skills with videos from Deke McClelland.

I started using Illustrator in 2009 when I got an interest in Graphic Design and knowing that I had to create Vector graphics for logo's and banners I used Deke McClelland's Illustrator one on one course and prgressed to the advanced tutorials.
Since this time I have graown very confident with creating company logo's with vector graphics and this is the software I have used for all my creations.

Knowing that Indesign was going to be needed to create brochure's and flyers I started to learn this software in 2011.
With this skill set I created a 52 page Undertaker interactive magazine which had some video highlights embedded in it. Creating such a huge piece of work gave me the confidence necessary to create brochure's, flyer's and other printable media.
